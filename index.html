About IDRD

A practical research institute for displacement and rural transformation.

IDRD generates evidence, policy analysis, and practical solutions to address displacement, rural poverty, food insecurity, undernutrition, climate shocks, and livelihood vulnerability.

The institute works at the intersection of humanitarian response and long-term development, supporting government institutions, development partners, universities, and communities with research, technical advice, training, and policy products.

Vision

To become a leading center of evidence, policy, and innovation for durable solutions and rural resilience in Somalia and the Horn of Africa.

Mission

To generate high-quality research, policy guidance, and capacity development that improves the lives of displaced and rural communities.

Approach

Evidence-driven, locally grounded, policy-oriented, and focused on practical implementation with government and development partners.

Thematic Areas

Our areas of work

Internal Displacement & Durable Solutions

IDP protection, settlement planning, land access, self-reliance, and long-term reintegration pathways.

Food Security & Nutrition

Household food insecurity, child undernutrition, maternal nutrition, and nutrition-sensitive programming.

Rural Livelihoods & Pastoral Mobility

Pastoral systems, smallholder agriculture, livestock recovery, rural markets, and livelihood diversification.

Climate Change & Resilience

Drought, floods, climate adaptation, water systems, rangeland degradation, and resilience investment.

Women & Children Vulnerability

Gendered impacts of displacement, child nutrition, education disruption, protection risks, and care burdens.

Policy Research & Capacity Development

Policy briefs, strategic frameworks, executive training, data analysis, and institutional strengthening.
